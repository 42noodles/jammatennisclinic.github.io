ABOUT US

Executive Team

Our executive team is made up of our head coaches, who lead classes and handle the majority of businesss operations.

Jason Zhang
Co-Founder + Head of Operations

Jason is the head coach at LAHS 1 and one of the founding members of JAMMA. He has played tennis for over 9 years, including #1 Doubles on the Mountain View High School Varsity Team last year, and brings a bubbly enthusiasm and joy to coaching. Focusing on not only the technical parts of tennis but also on the opportunities for personal growth tennis offers, Jason values bringing a positive impact to the students he coaches as a whole. When he's not on the courts, Jason focuses on overall company vision and helping the JAMMA team continue firing on all cylinders.

Megan Choy
Co-Founder + Director of Business

Megan is the head coach for the Blach 3 class and director of business at JAMMA. She plays singles on the Homestead High School Girl's Varsity Team, and has played tennis since she was was 10. She always has fun while playing and hopes to help other players have fun as well! As the treasurer of JAMMA, she manages and documents finances.

Pedram Bayat
Co-Founder + Director of Recruitment

Pedram is the head coach for the Cooper 1 class. He has been playing tennis for 6 years, and was the captain of the Mountain View High School JV Team last school year. As a coach, he works patiently with his students and focuses on individualized and direct feedback while maintaining a positive atmosphere during the lesson. He takes value in watching his students grow, not only in their tennis skills but also in their competitiveness, sportsmanship, and passion for tennis. Off the court, Pedram works with the JAMMA team to streamline student recruitment and grow the JAMMA community.

Archer Date
Co-Founder + Director of Leadership

Archer has been playing tennis for around nine years, and he is currently the head coach for the Cooper 4 + 5 classes. Last year, he played varsity doubles on the Mountain View High School tennis team, and he is excited to share what he's learned with his students! Overall, Archer believes that having a positive learning environment is one of the most important things to have while learning, so he works diligently with his team to make the lessons engaging and exciting in addition to educational. Seeing his students' skills grow through feedback tailored to each individual and their characters develop as they experience and overcome hardship (learning a new sport isn't easy!) is one of the most rewarding things Archer has experienced in his life. Outside of class, he works to improve the quality of JAMMA coaching as well as the leadership capabilities of each coach. He'd also love to convince you why biting cheese sticks is better than stringing them.
